about me

I am a keen and energised physicist currently completing my PhD at the University of Birmingham.

My research interests include:

  • the general behaviour of linear quantum systems,
  • applications of quantum systems to high-precision measurements, especially quantum noise,
  • designing detectors for new physics.

Additionally I am very interested in software development, and have an extensive github portfolio.

I am also a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration.

In my free time, I enjoy reading books, playing board games, and walking—the above cover photo was taken in the beautiful Atlas Mountains south of Marrakesh :)

projects

general realisation techniques for high-precision measurements

Recently I have been working to bring techniques developed by the quantum control community into the realm of high-precision measurements. Specifically a way to realise arbitrary linear quantum systems from their transfer functions. A paper I have written utilising this is currently at the final draft stage.

In the future I plan to apply these techniques to the realisation of second-order quantum filters for use in gravitational-wave detectors.

I am also currently writing a python module to automatically find the realisation from a given transfer function called SImBA.

design of next-generation gravitational wave interferometers

I am also interested in how the quantum noise of next-generation gravitational wave detectors can be improved, for example using novel quantum devices such as the unstable filter in novel new configurations as I did in publication 1 below.

control issues in unstable quantum systems

I am also interested in studying the control of unstable quantum systems, such as the unstable filter, and whether or not a stabilising controller can be achieved in cases where approximations break down, such as long cavities.
